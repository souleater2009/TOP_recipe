Slow Cooker Shrimp Boil

This slow cooker shrimp boil recipe has the flavor you love of a shrimp
boil, without the open steaming pot, a great way to keep the kitchen cool in
the summer. With seasoned shrimp, sausage, potatoes, and corn, it doesn't
get any better than this.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 1/2 pounds baby red potatoes, halved
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, cut in half
  • 1/4 cup seafood seasoning, such as Old Bay®, divided
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce, such as Crystal®, plus more to taste if desired
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 to 3 medium ears corn cut into 2-inch-thick rounds
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3/4 cup butter, divided
  • 2 pounds unpeeled shrimp
  • chopped fresh parsley, for serving (optional)
  • cocktail sauce and lemon wedges ,for serving

Directions

  1. Place onion, potatoes, and garlic in the bottom of a slow cooker and
    sprinkle with salt and pepper. Squeeze lemon halves over potatoes; add lemon
    halves in as well if desired. Add 3 tablespoons Old Bay, hot sauce,
    Worcestershire, and water and stir to combine. Add bay leaves, cover, and cook 1 to 1 1/2 hours on High or 2 1/2 hours on Low.
  2. Uncover and remove lemon halves if used and add in sausage and corn. Top
    with 1 stick butter. Cover and cook 2 hours on High or 4 hours on Low or
    until potatoes are tender.
  3. Stir in shrimp and top with remaining butter. Cook on High for 30 minutes,
    stirring half way through or until shrimp are cooked and everything is
    heated through.
  4. Serve topped with parsley and with lemon wedges and cocktail sauce.
